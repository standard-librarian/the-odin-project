Odin Recipes: Koshari

Description

Koshari, also spelled as Kushari or Koshary, is a popular Egyptian dish that has its roots in the country's diverse culinary history. It is considered one of Egypt's national dishes and is widely enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 1 cup brown or green lentils
  • 1 cup small elbow macaroni or short spaghetti noodles
  • 1 cup boiled chickpeas (canned or cooked from dried)
  • 2 cups tomato sauce (can use canned tomato sauce or homemade)
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Steps
  1. Rinse the rice and lentils separately under cold water until the water runs clear. Then, cook the rice and lentils separately according to their package instructions until they are both fully cooked. Add a pinch of salt to each while cooking for flavor.
  2. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until it is al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet or saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and saut√© for about 1 minute until fragrant. Be careful not to let it burn. Then, add the tomato sauce, season with salt and pepper, and simmer on low heat for about 10 minutes to let the flavors meld.
  4. In a separate small saucepan, heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the thinly sliced onions and cook, stirring frequently, until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove the onions from the oil and set them aside on a plate lined with a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  5. In a small bowl, mix the white vinegar with the minced garlic to create the garlic vinegar sauce. Set it aside.
  6. To assemble the koshari, start by layering the cooked rice, followed by the cooked lentils, pasta, and boiled chickpeas in a large serving dish or individual bowls.
  7. Generously drizzle the tomato sauce over the top of the layered ingredients, making sure to coat them well.
